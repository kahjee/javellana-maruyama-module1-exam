Christ the Redeemer Statue

The Christ the redeemer statue is an Art Deco statue that represents Jesus Christ located in Rio de Janeiro in Brazil. It was constructed between 1922 and 1931 and was created by the French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazillian engineer Heitor De Silva Costa in collaboration with French engineer Albert Caquot and Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida. The statue is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone and is 98 feet tall, excluding its 28 feet pedestal, with its arms stretching 92 feet across. It weighs around 700 tons and is located at the peak of the Corcovado Mountain. The statue serves as a symbol of Christianity around the world and has been one of the most recognizable things that represent Brazil as it was voted as one of the new seven wonders of the world.

Iguaçu Falls

Iguazu Falls, or Iguaçu Falls are waterfalls of the Iguazu River. Its name is derived from the Guarani or Tupi language, y, or i, meaning water, and uasu, meaning big. The waterfalls lie on the border of Argentina’s Misiones and Brazil’s Paraná. Legend has it that a woman named Naipí was chosen to be sacrificed on the Iguazu rivers to a god named Mboi, however she fled with a warrior named Tarobá. Upon hearing this, Mboi was enraged and moved his body with fury, the force of which created the falls. The longest drop is over 82 meters, which is taller than Niagra Falls. Once you arrive at the falls, the sheer size and the breathtaking view are sure to be an unforgettable sight to behold.

São Paulo Museum of Art

The São Paulo Museum of Art is an art museum located near the heart of São Paulo. It is a concrete and glass structure that is designed by Lina Bo Bardi, an Italian-Brazilian architect that was devoted to promote the cultural potential of architecture. It houses numerous collections of Latin American and European art, as well as pieces of African and Asian art, amounting to over eight thousand art pieces. It was also one of the first Brazilian museums that hosted Post-World War II artworks and pieces. This symbol of modern Brazilian architecture is sure to inspire anyone who visits.
